NeonSFF

3D Printed Computer Case

NeonSFF is a 3D printed computer case designed in Solidworks. This is one of the most intensive projects I have completed after building my 3D printer with over 95% of the case comprised of PLA plastic. Despite being under 6 liters, the case does not compromise performance with room for a discrete GPU and two 2.5” SDDs. The current model has been in use for over a year now with no issues whatsoever.

The Artist (Automaton)

NJ TSA 2017: 1st Place in Animatronics

For our final year, my team and I decided on an ambitious project for the animatronics event of the TSA competition. The main project goal was to build an automaton capable of mimicking human behavior such as blicking, looking, and speaking. The project evolved with speech recognition and drawing capabilities. As a result, the project combined a variety of technologies such as Arduino, Raspberry Pi, and CMU Sphinx and systems such as hydraulics. We ended our time competing in TSA by taking first place in the state for the second year in a row.

Animatronic Hand

NJ TSA 2016: 1st Place in Animatronics

For our first TSA animatronics project, we decided to create an animatronic hand that shadowed the user’s hand movements in real time. We achieved this through utilizing flex sensors which measure the bend of the user’s fingers. The Arduino powering the project translating the analog values from these sensors to a PWM signal to the servos that controlled the animatronic hand. That first year, we did not place because we had failed to incorporate the other scoring criteria. We knew that the project idea itself was good and the next year we redesign the hand from the ground up. As a result, we took 1st place in the animatronics event.

3D Printer

June 2017

I built this printer from scratch to get into 3D printing to design custom parts for future projects. However, 3D printing soon changed from simply a hobby to a passion. Built upon the Arduino Mega microcontroller and the open source RAMPS 1.4 board, the printer was designed to be low cost while still achieving decent print quality and print volume. This led to the decision of having a cantilever design and utilizing 3D printed parts. The printer is currently running Repetier, an open source 3D Printer firmware.

Timelapse Camera

September 2017

Four days prior to the 2017 New York Maker Faire, I decided I wanted to create a timelapse of the entire event. I could have just used my phone, but that would prevent me from using it for the entire day. So out of the spirit of Maker Faire, I set out to create a timelapse camera built upon the Raspberry Pi Zero and Raspberry Pi Camera. Based on the wearable timelapse camera by Adafruit, I redesigned the case to be thinner since I didn’t need a battery as it was powered by an external battery pack, included a magnetic shirt mount, and a detachable camera lens mount.

Power Method

December 2018

For the final project for my linear algebra class, my group decided to implement the power method in Python. The power method is used to approximate the singular value decomposition (SVD) of a matrix which is necessary for many applications where the data sets are simply too large to efficiently calculate the actual SVD. The power method (and its variations) are used in a variety of applications such as PCA and PageRank.
